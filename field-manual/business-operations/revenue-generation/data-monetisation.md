# Data Monetisation

For an MSP/MSSP, data is usually a cost centre (storage, ingestion, analysis). Data Monetisation is the process of flipping this model. By leveraging the unique high-level view of multiple client environments, an organisation can create proprietary Intellectual Property (IP) that provides value far beyond a standard monthly service fee.

***

## "Golden Rules" of Data Monetisation

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Rule 1: Anonymisation is Non-Negotiable

Under UK GDPR, we must ensure data is stripped of PII (Personally Identifiable Information). We are selling the _trend_, never the _target_.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Rule 2: The "Contractual Right to Aggregate"

The Master Service Agreement (MSA) must explicitly state that we own the Aggregated Metadata derived from the service. Without this, monetisation becomes legally dubious.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Rule 3: Trust is the Primary Asset

If a client feels their data is being "mined" rather than "protected," the relationship is dead. Transparency is key - explain that data aggregation improves their own security via "herd immunity."
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

***

## Threat Intelligence Exchange

MSSPs sit on a diverse range of attack vectors that software vendors and threat intelligence platforms (TIPs) are desperate to see.

* **Zero-Day Discovery & Malware Samples:** If your SOC identifies a new strain of ransomware or a unique C2 (Command & Control) infrastructure, that data has immediate market value.
* **The "Vendor Credit" Model:** Rather than cash, many MSSPs monetise data by feeding anonymised telemetry back to vendors (e.g., SentinelOne, Fortinet, or CrowdStrike) in exchange for enhanced partner tiers, marketing development funds (MDF), or licensing discounts.
* **Proprietary Feeds:** High-maturity MSSPs package their own "Community Threat Feed" based on what they see across their specific client base, which can be sold as a premium add-on to other security teams.

***

## Macro-Level Industry Benchmarking

Clients may ask, _"How do we compare to our peers?"_ The data holds the answer.

* **The "State of the Sector" Report:** By aggregating anonymised security scores across specific verticals (e.g., "The 2026 Legal Sector Threat Landscape"), we create a high-value marketing asset.
* **Benchmarking as a Service:** We can charge clients for a "Maturity Benchmark" report. This uses data from the entire portfolio to show a client exactly where they over-invest or under-invest compared to their direct competitors.
* **Commercial Insight:** Selling aggregated, non-identifiable trends (e.g., "SaaS adoption rates in UK Manufacturing") to market research firms or Private Equity groups looking for due diligence data.

***

## Risk Underwriting & Insurance Partnerships

Cyber insurers are moving away from "self-assessment" questionnaires and toward "data-backed" underwriting.

* **Actuarial Data sets:** Insurers want to know which stacks (e.g., MFA + EDR + Managed Firewall) actually result in fewer claims. By providing anonymised "Loss Ratio" data, an MSSP can position itself as a Preferred Provider.
* **The Referral Engine:** Instead of selling the data for a one-off fee, we "monetise" it by securing lower insurance premiums for clients, which justifies a higher management fee for our services.

***

## Productivity & Workforce Analytics

Standard MSP tools (RMM, M365 usage reports) track how a workforce actually functions.

* **The Asset:** Aggregated data on software adoption (e.g., "The client pays for 200 Adobe licenses but only 40 are opened weekly").
* **Monetisation:** Selling "Licensing Optimisation" audits or providing "Workplace Efficiency" benchmarks to clients. We are effectively selling back the money we save them.

***

## Infrastructure Lifecycle Benchmarking

General MSPs have the data to know exactly when specific hardware models begin to fail or underperform.

* **The Asset:** Hardware reliability data across 1,000s of endpoints (e.g., "Laptop Brand X has a 15% higher failure rate in year 3 than Brand Y").
* **Monetisation:** This data is incredibly valuable to Hardware Vendors or Insurance Underwriters who cover business equipment. You can leverage this "Field Reliability" data for better procurement pricing or direct referral fees.
