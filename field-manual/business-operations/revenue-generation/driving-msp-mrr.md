# Driving MSP MRR

Increasing the Financial Resilience of an MSP is directly proportional to its ratio of Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) vs. Non-Recurring Revenue (NRR). High MRR allows for better capacity planning, easier access to credit, and higher business valuation. This article outlines strategies to convert "one-off" technical tasks into sustained service tiers.

***

## The "Subscription-First" Mindset

Every one-off project should be viewed as a "gateway" to a recurring service.

* **Project-to-Service Mapping:** Never sell a "Firewall Installation" (one-off) without an attached "Managed Firewall Service" (recurring). The installation becomes the "Onboarding Fee," while the monitoring becomes the "Annuity."
* **Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS):** Instead of a client paying £10,000 for a server refresh, offer the hardware, licensing, and support for a flat monthly fee over 36 months. This lowers the client's barrier to entry while locking in a long-term contract.
* **The "Security Stack" Bundle:** Move away from "à la carte" billing. Bundle EDR, Backup, MFA, and Patch Management into a single "Per User" seat price. It is harder for a client to cancel a "Security Seat" than it is to cancel a single line item.

## Contractual Mechanisms for Longevity

"Staying power" is also about the legal "stickiness" of our revenue.

* **Multi-Year Agreements:** Shift from 12-month to 36-month contracts with annual price escalations (e.g., RPI + 3%) built-in. This protects our margins against inflation.
* **Evergreen Clauses:** Ensure contracts auto-renew unless a formal notice is given 90 days prior. This prevents revenue "gaps" caused by administrative oversight.
* **Service Level Agreements (SLAs):** Tie recurring fees to a high-quality SLA. Clients are less likely to churn if we are meeting a contractual uptime or response guarantee that is vital to their business operations.

***

## Key Metrics for Staying Power

To manage recurring revenue, you must track these three "North Star" metrics:

1. **MRR Concentration:** Ensure no single client represents more than 15-20% of your total recurring revenue. This prevents a "Concentration Risk" where one cancellation sinks the business.
2. **Net Revenue Retention (NRR):** This measures how much our existing recurring revenue grows (via upselling) vs. how much is lost (via churn). Aim for >100%.
3. **Cost to Serve (CTS):** Monitoring the profit margin on our MRR. If a recurring contract requires 20 hours of manual labour a month, it isn't "Passive Income" - it’s a resource drain.
