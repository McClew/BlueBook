# Role & Skills

## The Role

A NSCS Cyber Advisor provides SMEs with reliable, cost-effective, and practical security advice. Unlike an Auditor, a Cyber Advisor acts as a consultant who bridges the cap between strict technical standards and the day-to-day realities of running a small business.

The Advisor is there to help them understand _**why**_ these controls matter and _**how**_ to implement them without breaking their business operations.

***

## Core Duties

Cyber Advisors are expected to perform the following key duties:

* **Condudct Cyber Essentials Gap Analysis:** Assess an organisations internet-facing IT to identify where they meet and fail to meet CE controls.
* **Develop & Present Reports:** Prepare reports targeted at senior leadership detailing met and unmet requirements. Crucially, an Advisor must explain _**why**_ a control is unmet, the _**risks**_ to the business and recommend _**actionable**_ steps.
* **Agree on Remediation Activities:** Work collaboratively with the business leaders and IT teams to agree on a remediation plan.
* **Plan Sympathetically:** Ensure remediation activities align with the business's risk appetite and priorities, minimising disruption to their daily operations.
* **Implement & Guide Remediation:** Either directly implement the fixes or guide the clients technical teams in doing so.
* **Post-Remediation Report:** Develop and present a final report detailing the status of the controls after the work is complete.

***

## Required Knowledge

To succeed as an Advisor, one must havea deep understanding of:&#x20;

* **The Cyber Essentials Standard:** An Advisor needs to fully understand the five technical controls: Firewalls, Secure Configuration, User Access Control, Malware Protection and Security Update Management.
* **The NCSC Small Organisation Guide:** An Advisor must be able to transalte CE requirements into the plain-English concepts.
* **Small Business IT:** An Advisor must have a deep understanding of typical small business environments, including cloud servers, basic networking and BYOD setups.

***

## Key Skills

As well as technical knowledge, Advisors must be able to effectively:&#x20;

* **Communicate:** Communicating complex cyber security issues simply and non-technically to senior business leaders.
* **Actively Listen:** Asking the right questions to extract network and device information from non-technical staff.
* **Write Reports:** Consucting logical, professional reports using language appropriate for the target audience.
* **Present Findings:** Delivering findings succinctly and managing client pushbacks effectively.
* **Business Empathy:** Understanding the business priorities of the client and planning work in a way that minimises disruption.

***

## Required Behaviours

The Cyber Advisor standard mandatres three core behaviours when interacting with clients:

* **Professional Approach:** Following the professional code of conduct and ethical standards defined by the UK Cyber Security Council.
* **Collaborative Approach:** Working with the customer or third-parties as a partner to achieve their security objectives, rather than talking down to them.
* **Non-Judgemental:** Approaching the customer's current IT setup with an open mind, without incorporating prejudicial views.
